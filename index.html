Tic‑Tac‑Toe

Infinite Tic-Tac-Toe uses only three pieces per player: placing a fourth makes the first one disappear. The goal is to align three symbols to win each round. Matches are fast and strategic, and the first player to win 5 rounds claims overall victory in the game.

Connect Four

Connect 4 pits the player against a bot that drops its token into the gridto form a line of four. Each round requires smart planning and good timing. Matches play out quickly, and the first competitor to secure 5 victories wins to entire game.

Pong

Pong puts the player against a bot that tries to return the ball with precision. Each rally becomes faster, demanding sharp reflexes and control. The objective is simple: score five points before the opponent. The first to reach 5 goals wins the entire match.

Simon Says

Simon challenges the player with an ever‑growing sequence of lights and sounds. The goal is to repeat the pattern perfectly to advance to the next round. There is no bot here : only your memory, focus, and sense of rhythm determine how far you can progress through the game.

Snake

Snake challenges the player to guide a growing serpent that lengthens with every apple eaten. The difficulty rises as space becomes tighter, demanding precision and anticipation. The goal is simple: survive as long as possible by controlling the snake’s movement and avoiding any collision.
